Physical description of the American beaver

Bodysize

The American beaver is the largest rodent in North America. It is the Second-largest rodent in the world(alongside the European beaver ), first being the monstrous South American capybara . The length of their body varies anywhere from 29-35 in, with the tail adding another 7.9-13.8 in.

The adult American beaver usually weigh anywhere between 24-71 lb, with 44 lb being the average weight. They seem to conform to Bergmann's Rule : average weight of 37 lb in Ohio, 40 lb in Finland, and 42 lb in New York.

They have two different kinds of fur: long coarse hairs on the outer layer, and short fine hairs on the inner layer.

Appendages

Tail

The American beaver has a large black paddle-like tail. The tail is mostly hairless and covered with large scales. If it feels threatened it is found to slap its large tail against the ground/water.

Legs

The American beaver has short furry legs and black webbed feet, with five digits on each foot.

The front paws are more dextrous-primarily used for digging, folding leaves while eating, and twisting off small twigs as they gnaw at bark. Their hind feet are mostly webbed with the exeption of their first and second toes. This is thought to help with grooming.

Mouth and Teeth

The American beaver has the struggle of ever-growing incisors, meaning, their teeth never stop growing. They gnaw on bark mainly to keep these teeth from getting too long. While underwater, the beaver can close their lips beind their front teeth, allowing them to gnaw things even while underwater!

Range

The American beaver lives primarily in areas like ponds, rivers, lakes, and streams all across the USA; other than the more desert filled areas. After being near hunted to extinction, The American Beaver species was reintroduced to places like Spain, Great Britain, and some areas in China and Mongolia.

The beavers home is known as a lodge. It is primarily made up of branches, sticks, mud, and tends to have an opening accessible only from under the water. While beavers tend to build lodges, they tend to make dams much more often. They can even change the entire flow of water or create a whole new body of water, making them one of the few mammals capable of actively changing an ecosystem.

Subspecies of the American beaver

Once believed to be up to 25 seperate subspecies of The American beaver, it has since become difficult to determine the true numbers because of the large scale genetic mixing that has taken place; This is mainly due to the mass 'reintroduction' efforts with the intent to help the species recover their numbers.

History of the American beaver

The first ever record of The American beaver is from Germany 10-12 million years ago. The first record in North America is from Dayvile, Oregon 7 million years ago; two beaver teeth were found there. It is thought that they migrated to North America across the Bering Straight .

The American beaver was previously introduced into Patagonia South America, and Finland + Karelia in Europe. They have often been hunted for a chemical called "castoreum". Castoreum is an oily substance secreted by the 'castor glands' which are located near the cloaca. This chemical(along with a few others) would be refined mainly into vanity items like perfumes.

When fur hats came onto the fashion scene, people turned more often to beavers for thier beautiful fur. For this purpose it was near hunted to extinction. The real saving grace for their species as a whole was the sudden change in fashion style; fur hats died off, so people turned their attention from the beaver. Since then, the American beaver as a species has recovered exceptionally well throughout Europe. They have been reintroduced to other areas across the world too, however, The American Beaver's population in Europe is flourishing so much so that it alone was reason enough for them to be taken off the endangered species list.
